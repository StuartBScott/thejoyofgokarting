How to get started in Karting

Go Karting is a fun sport, suitable for a wide age group from 8year to 80years and beyond. To get started contact your local public Karting place. There are Petrol motored Kart's and Electric Kart's with venues that are outdoor and indoor. You can hire a Kart and race against friends, family and colleagues. You can book a 10 minute session, or multiple sessions. The venues will supply a helmet, balaclava and Kart. Now it's up to you, Go Racing.

Source: Poweplay.

Learn to Race

Some Karting venues offer a training progran for young drivers looking to get serious about Go Karting and improve their skills. The LeMans Drivers Academy offers a structured junior training progran for ages 8 - 18 helping them to develop real racecraft skills and build confidence behind the wheel. Through Expert coaching young drivers spend 30 minutes in a classroom discussion the fundamentals of Karting. These include braking techniques, passing, racing lines, kart control, consistency and more. This is followed by a 30 minute on track session to put what they have learnt to the test. This brings purpose to every lap and all laps are timed to show measureable improvement.

Source: LeMans Entertainment.

Racing Leagues

Go Karting is a Grassroot's motorsport. Join a Racing Club and you can get your Karting Australia Licence. Once you have this you can compete in Home Track Competitions, Neighbouring track events, or racines across your Stateand across Australia. It's high Octane entertainment the whole family can enjoy. Cadets start at age 6, with 12 months of practice and racing starts from age 7. The cadets race in smaller karts with less powerful engines. Then step up to Junios 12 years to 15 years, with full size karts and faster engines. Then Seniors starts at 16+. At the Karting Australia Website you will find links to clubs and other ways to get involved. Happy Karting race enthusiasts. See you on the track.

Source: Karting Australia
